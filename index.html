About Me

About me

I started a trip in 2019 that changed the direction of my life. My name is Khoula, and I am a Pakistani refugee. Being forced to leave our country due to religious restrictions and discrimination that had encroached on our lives was not a choice; it was a necessity. I was a level 9 student in Pakistan, pursuing my education and nurturing hopes of a bright future. However, life took a twist when my family and I relocated to Malaysia.

We moved into our new house, I began to have doubts about the educational prospects available in this country. Many people informed me that it is difficult to find a good education and employment possibilities. This pessimistic view made my ambitions seem unsure and depressing. But deep down, I was more motivated than ever to learn new things and to change my destiny. I started looking for possibilities that might restore my passion for my education because I was determined not to let the negativity get to me.

I came upon the IGCSE  exams while looking at several educational options. I was excited and full of new optimism at the idea of finally finishing high school. I took it upon myself to study for these tests as I didn't have access to a formal education or tuition. I chose five subjects—Math, English, Science, and randomly IT and Accounting, both of which were completely new to me. It seems like universe choose these subjects for me. Although the path was surely difficult, my constant determination and desire kept me moving forward. I used online tools, consumed YouTube videos, and asked friends who were still in school to lend me books. At last, my hard work paid off as I got some really good marks.

My goals for higher education were set after I passed my IGCSE. However, it was difficult to find good, cheap colleges in Malaysia. The little solutions offered were frequently unaffordable. I didn't let it stop me, so I chose to enroll in a respected online college in the UK to earn a Level 4 and 5 diploma. But despite my academic success, I had a difficult time finding jobs.

In the midst of my tireless search for opportunities, a message found me. It shared with me about a school called Sigma that provided training in web development and full-stack coding along with the confidence of job placement. Sigma felt like a beacon of light in the surrounding darkness as a refugee going through an environment of hardship and limited choices. I have my own quote that says "If you want to change your destiny as a refugee, align your career with the opportunities available to you. Accept every opportunity that comes your way". This belief drove me to learn more about Sigma and later I applied for a scholarship in expectation of the doors it would open for me in web development and beyound.
